## broker dealer & ATS

broker dealers operates an ATS under SEC Reg ATS. These ATS functions as a dark pool -- an off-exchange venus that internalizes order flow
& crosses client orders privately before routing residual flow to lit exchanges

Requirements before HFT/ Quant firms can do this:
1. firm registers as a broker dealer entity. this is required to operate the ATS
2. FINRA oversees the broker-dealer, SEC oversees the ATS registration

* lit exchange - transparent, public trading venue where buy & sell orders are openly displayed in an order book. (examples include NYSE, NASDAQ etc.)
* SEC - federal geovernmet agency created by congress, & it protects investors in the US by regulating the securities market
    enforces securities law
    & ensures all info to investors is above board
    & ensures all players (in the financial markets) act according to the law
* FINRA - a private self-regulatory oranization (SRO) and is a not-for-profit organization overseen by the SEC.
    it seeks to protect investors by overseeing all brokers in the securities marketplace
    lays out specific laws & requirements & provides certificates for individuals to operate in the securities market
* SEC vs FINRA:
    - FINRA is overseen by SEC. FINRA's scope is narrower, concentrating on broker-dealer compliance & investor protections with securities trading
    - when insider trading happens, SEC is generally in charge
        - FINRA typically notices first, it has surveillance systems that monitor trading. flags & then refers case to SEC
        - once referred (or when SEC detects it themselves), SEC investigates & prosecutes
